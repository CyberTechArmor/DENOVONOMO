# Patient Communication

## What Is This?

Patient communication platforms are software systems that enable two-way messaging between a primary care practice and its patients via SMS text, email, secure chat, and sometimes voice. These platforms go beyond the basic appointment reminder to encompass the full spectrum of non-clinical communication: appointment confirmations and reminders, post-visit follow-ups, recall and reactivation campaigns, broadcast messages, prescription notifications, referral coordination, billing communications, and general patient inquiries.

The category has expanded dramatically since 2020. Where practices once relied on phone calls and patient portal messages (which fewer than 30% of patients actively use), modern communication platforms meet patients where they already are --- their text messages and email inboxes. Purpose-built healthcare communication platforms like Klara, Luma Health, OhMD, Artera (formerly WELL Health), and Relatient handle message routing, HIPAA-compliant content management, staff workflow automation, and EHR integration.

The critical distinction from a general texting service is HIPAA compliance. Sending a text message that says "Your lab results are ready" to a patient's phone number is technically a HIPAA disclosure --- the message could be seen by anyone with access to the phone. Healthcare communication platforms manage this through consent workflows, message content controls (avoiding PHI in SMS, directing patients to a secure link for details), and documentation of patient communication preferences.

These platforms typically cost $200--$500 per provider per month, though some charge per-message ($0.01--$0.05/message) or per-patient-per-month. The ROI is driven by reduced no-show rates (text reminders reduce no-shows by 25--40%), staff time savings (replacing phone calls with automated messages), and improved patient retention through engagement.

## Why Does a Primary Care Practice Need This?

**Phone tag is destroying productivity.** The average primary care practice receives 50--150 phone calls per day. Staff spend 30--60 minutes per provider per day on outbound calls: appointment reminders, callback requests, referral follow-ups, and prescription notifications. Most of these calls go to voicemail. Asynchronous text and email communication eliminates the back-and-forth, with patients responding on their own schedule.

**No-show reduction.** Patient no-shows cost the average primary care practice $150,000--$200,000 annually in lost revenue. Text-based appointment reminders with easy confirmation/cancellation options reduce no-show rates by 25--40%. Automated reminders sent 48 hours and 2 hours before appointments are significantly more effective than a single reminder call.

**Patient retention and recall.** Patients who are overdue for annual wellness visits, chronic care follow-ups, or preventive screenings represent both a clinical risk and a revenue opportunity. Automated recall campaigns via text and email re-engage these patients at scale, recovering visits that would otherwise be lost to attrition.

**Competitive differentiation.** Patients rate communication ease as a top-three factor in practice satisfaction. A practice that responds to patient inquiries within 30 minutes via text creates a fundamentally different patient experience than one that requires a phone call during business hours and returns messages the next day.

**Staff satisfaction.** Front-desk and nursing staff consistently cite phone volume as a top source of burnout. Shifting communication to asynchronous text-based channels reduces interruptions, allows batched responses, and distributes communication workload more evenly.

## How to Decide If You Need It

You need a patient communication platform if:

- Your phone system handles more than 30 incoming calls per provider per day
- Your no-show rate exceeds 8%
- Your patient portal message adoption is below 40%
- You lack automated appointment reminders (or use only voice-based reminders)
- Staff spend more than 30 minutes per day per provider on outbound phone calls
- You want to implement recall campaigns but lack the tool to do so at scale
- Patients complain about difficulty reaching your office

You may not need a dedicated platform if:

- Your EHR's built-in communication tools (athenahealth Communicate, Epic MyChart messaging) meet your needs and are well-adopted by patients
- You are a concierge or DPC practice with a small panel (under 500 patients) where personal phone communication is part of the value proposition
- Your patient population has very low SMS/email adoption (rare, but possible in some communities)

## Order of Operations

1. **Audit current communication volume and channels.** Track inbound and outbound calls for one week by category: appointment-related, clinical questions, billing questions, prescription questions, referral questions, other. Measure average response time for patient inquiries.

2. **Define communication categories.** Map which communications should be automated (appointment reminders, recall, balance notifications), which should be templated (post-visit instructions, referral notifications), and which require live staff interaction (clinical questions, complex scheduling).

3. **Select a platform with EHR integration.** The platform must integrate with your EHR/PMS for appointment data (to send reminders), patient demographics (for contact information), and ideally clinical data (for targeted outreach like "patients due for A1c"). Without integration, staff manually manage message lists --- defeating the purpose.

4. **Implement appointment reminders first.** This has the fastest, most measurable ROI. Configure automated text and email reminders at 72 hours, 24 hours, and 2 hours before appointments. Include easy confirm/cancel/reschedule options.

5. **Add two-way messaging.** Enable patients to text the practice for general inquiries. Route inbound messages to appropriate staff (clinical questions to nurses, scheduling to front desk, billing to billing). Establish response time targets (under 30 minutes during business hours).

6. **Deploy recall and outreach campaigns.** Build automated campaigns for patients overdue for annual wellness visits, chronic disease follow-ups, and preventive screenings.

7. **Add broadcast messaging.** Configure the ability to send mass messages for practice announcements (holiday hours, new providers, flu shot availability, weather closures).

8. **Train staff and establish workflows.** Define who monitors and responds to each message category. Establish escalation protocols for urgent messages. Train staff on HIPAA-compliant messaging practices.

## Options by Practice Size

**Solo/Small Practice (1--2 providers)**
OhMD (free tier for basic two-way texting; $150--$250/month for full features), Klara ($250--$350/month), or your EHR's built-in messaging. One staff member manages the inbox. Budget: $0--$350/month.

**Small Group (3--5 providers)**
Klara ($250--$400/provider/month), Luma Health ($250--$400/provider/month), or OhMD Professional ($200--$300/provider/month). Shared team inboxes with routing rules. Automated reminders and basic recall campaigns. Budget: $600--$2,000/month.

**Medium Group (6--15 providers)**
Artera (formerly WELL Health), Luma Health, or Relatient. Enterprise features including multi-location management, advanced campaign builder, waitlist management, and analytics. Budget: $2,000--$6,000/month.

**Large Group (15+ providers)**
Artera, Luma Health, or Relatient Enterprise. API-driven integration, custom workflows, population outreach, patient satisfaction surveying, and dedicated account management. Negotiate volume pricing. Budget: $4,000--$12,000/month.

## Vendor Landscape

**Klara** has established a strong position in the small to mid-size primary care market. Their platform emphasizes simplicity and staff workflow efficiency, with a unified inbox that aggregates patient messages from text, phone, web, and fax into a single interface. Klara's team-based routing ensures messages reach the right staff member without patients navigating complex phone trees. Their messaging is designed to keep PHI off SMS by directing patients to a secure web-based conversation for clinical details.

**Luma Health** positions itself as the "patient success platform," going beyond messaging to include online scheduling, waitlist management, referral management, and operational analytics. Luma's strength is its breadth of functionality and deep EHR integrations (60+ systems). For practices wanting a single platform for patient engagement rather than multiple point solutions, Luma offers the most comprehensive feature set.

**OhMD** occupies the value segment with a free tier that includes basic two-way texting --- enough for a solo practice to get started. Their paid tiers add video visits, automation, and broadcast messaging. OhMD's simplicity is both a strength (easy to adopt) and a limitation (less automation sophistication than Klara or Luma).

**Artera** (rebranded from WELL Health in 2023, not to be confused with the Canadian company WELL Health Technologies) targets larger organizations with enterprise communication orchestration. Their platform manages the entire patient communication lifecycle across multiple channels, locations, and departments. Artera's "smart" messaging uses patient preference data and behavioral signals to optimize message timing and channel selection.

**Relatient** (acquired by Arrive Health, now part of the Phreesia ecosystem) provides automated patient communication with particular strength in appointment management, waitlist backfill, and patient satisfaction surveying. Their integration with Phreesia's broader patient intake platform creates a comprehensive patient engagement stack.

**Emerging trends:** AI-powered triage of inbound patient messages (routing based on content analysis), conversational AI for handling routine inquiries (appointment scheduling, prescription refill requests), multilingual messaging with real-time translation, and integration with remote patient monitoring for proactive outreach based on clinical data.

## Compliance & Regulatory Notes

**HIPAA and SMS.** Standard SMS is not encrypted and can be viewed by anyone with access to the phone. HIPAA does not explicitly prohibit SMS, but practices must manage risk. Best practices: (1) obtain documented patient consent for text communication, (2) avoid including PHI in SMS messages (use generic messages like "You have a new message from Dr. Smith's office" with a link to a secure portal for details), (3) provide opt-out mechanisms, and (4) document patient communication preferences in the chart.

**TCPA compliance.** The Telephone Consumer Protection Act (TCPA) regulates automated text and voice messages. Practices must obtain prior express written consent before sending automated texts. Consent should specify the types of messages (appointment reminders, health information, billing), the phone number, and the right to revoke consent. TCPA violations carry penalties of $500--$1,500 per message. Healthcare communication platforms typically manage consent collection, but the practice bears ultimate responsibility.

**CAN-SPAM Act.** Email communications must comply with CAN-SPAM: include the practice's physical address, provide unsubscribe functionality, and honor opt-out requests within 10 business days. Healthcare communications to existing patients are generally exempt from some CAN-SPAM provisions, but best practice is full compliance.

**State-specific texting laws.** Some states have additional consent requirements for automated messaging. Florida's mini-TCPA, California's restrictions, and other state laws may impose stricter consent or opt-out requirements.

**Documentation requirements.** Patient communications may become part of the medical record, particularly if they involve clinical content, care instructions, or medication information. Ensure your communication platform integrates with or archives to the EHR for documentation continuity.

**10DLC registration.** Since 2023, mobile carriers require businesses sending SMS through standard 10-digit numbers to register with The Campaign Registry (TCR) for Application-to-Person (A2P) messaging. Healthcare communication platforms handle this registration, but practices using DIY texting solutions must register or risk message filtering and delivery failures.

## Common Mistakes

**Sending PHI via text.** A text that says "Your chlamydia test was negative" violates HIPAA and patient privacy. Configure your platform to send notification-only messages ("You have a new message from your doctor") with a link to secure content. Train staff to never include diagnoses, test results, or treatment details in SMS.

**Not obtaining proper consent.** Sending automated texts without documented TCPA consent exposes the practice to significant liability. Collect consent during patient registration --- most check-in platforms can incorporate text consent into the intake workflow.

**Over-messaging patients.** Practices that enable every possible automated message create "message fatigue." Patients who receive an appointment confirmation, a 72-hour reminder, a 24-hour reminder, a 2-hour reminder, and a post-visit survey within a single care episode will opt out. Limit automated messages to 2--3 per appointment cycle.

**Not monitoring response times.** Enabling two-way texting creates an expectation of timely response. If patients text your office and receive no response for hours, the platform does more harm than good. Establish and publish response time expectations (e.g., "We respond to messages within 2 hours during business hours") and staff the inbox accordingly.

**Using personal cell phones for patient communication.** Staff texting patients from personal phones creates HIPAA violations (no encryption, no documentation, no BAA with the phone carrier) and boundary issues. All patient communication must flow through the practice's HIPAA-compliant platform.

**Neglecting recall campaigns.** Most practices implement appointment reminders (easy, immediate ROI) but never configure recall campaigns (which require more setup). Recall campaigns for overdue wellness visits, chronic care follow-ups, and preventive screenings drive significant revenue and improve clinical quality measures.

## Recommended Implementation Timeline

**Weeks 1--2:** Audit communication patterns. Track call volume by category, measure response times, calculate no-show rates, and document current reminder and recall processes.

**Weeks 3--4:** Evaluate platforms. Demo Klara, Luma Health, and OhMD (or whichever three match your size and needs). Assess EHR integration, automation capabilities, and staff workflow. Check references from similar-sized practices.

**Weeks 5--6:** Select platform and execute contract. Negotiate pricing, confirm BAA execution, and begin technical integration with EHR/PMS.

**Weeks 7--8:** Configure appointment reminders. Set up automated text and email reminders at optimal intervals (72h and 24h, or 48h and 2h --- test what works for your population). Configure confirmation/cancellation workflows.

**Week 9:** Staff training on two-way messaging. Define routing rules (which staff handles which message categories), response time expectations, and PHI-safe messaging protocols.

**Week 10:** Launch Phase 1 --- appointment reminders and two-way messaging. Monitor no-show rates, message volume, and response times daily.

**Weeks 11--12:** Launch Phase 2 --- recall campaigns. Build campaigns for patients overdue for annual wellness visits and chronic care follow-ups. Start with a small cohort (100 patients) to test messaging effectiveness before scaling.

**Weeks 13--16:** Launch Phase 3 --- broadcast messaging and advanced workflows. Configure post-visit satisfaction surveys, balance notification messages, and practice announcement broadcasts.

**Week 17--20:** Optimization. Analyze message engagement rates, opt-out rates, and no-show trends. Adjust messaging frequency and timing based on data. Expand recall campaigns to additional patient populations.

**Ongoing:** Weekly review of message inbox management and response times. Monthly no-show rate tracking. Quarterly recall campaign performance analysis. Annual platform review and contract negotiation.
